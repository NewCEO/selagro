    TREE CROPS

    Raw cashew nut

    Cashew (Anacardium Occidentale L.) is a tree crop of considerable economic importance to Nigeria and other tropical countries. Delicately sweet yet crunchy and delicious cashew nut packed with energy, antioxidants, minerals and vitamins that are essential for robust health. Cashew, or “caju” in Portuguese, is one of the popular ingredients in sweet as well savory dishes worldwide. Apart from being a source of useful products and by-products for food, medicinal and industrial applications, cashew also gives a useful shade, while ornamental and alley trees are suitable for the control of soil erosion, particularly for the protection of watersheds and dams. In addition to this, cashew nutshell liquid (CNSL), a by-product of processing cashew, is mostly composed of anacardic acids; which has been used effectively against tooth abscesses because of their lethality to bacteria. They are rich in “heart-friendly” monounsaturated fatty acids like oleic, and palmitoleic acids. These essential fatty acids help lower harmful LDL-cholesterol while increasing good HDL cholesterol in the blood. Cashew nuts are abundant sources of essential minerals.

    Sheanut

    Commonly known as Shea Tree (Vitellaria paradoxa) is indigenous to Africa. The shea region in Nigeria is responsible for the highest production of shea nuts in the world with an estimated shea nut harvest of 500,00 metric tonnes/per year, which is equivalent to approximately 50% of the total shea yield in Africa. The shea fruit consists of a thin, tart, nutritious pulp that surrounds a relatively large, oil-rich seed from which shea butter is extracted. The Bark is used for medicinal purposes, its trunk makes excellent cooking fuel and wood for construction. The butter extracted from the fruit can be used for medicine and cosmetic purposes, it is also used in chocolate making factories and pastry production where it is used as a substitute for cocoa butter.

    Cocoa

    The cocoa bean (Theobroma cacao L) is the dried and fully fermented seed of Theobroma cacao, from which cocoa solids and cocoa butter can be extracted. The vast majority of cocoa farms are not owned by the companies that make chocolate products or supply cocoa. From the processed cocoa bean comes the fluid paste, or liquor, from which cocoa powder and chocolate are made. Chocolate is sold directly to the consumer as solid bars of eating chocolate, as packaged cocoa, used as a coating for candy bars, bulk chocolates, flavours in such “chocolate” products as syrups, toppings, chocolate liquor, chocolate milk, prepared cake mix, baking chocolate and in pharmaceuticals.. It is also used by confectioners as coating for many types of cookies and cakes, and by ice-cream companies as coating for frozen novelties.

    Gum Arabic

    Gum Arabic (Acacia Senegal) is a natural gum harvested from the exterior of Acacia trees in the form of dry, hard nodules up to 50 mm in diameter, and ranging from almost colourless to brown. It is a soluble fibre grown in sub-Saharan Africa. It is one of the most important medicinal plants used in traditional, or alternative, medicine. It has been confirmed to have several therapeutic actions, such as being hypoglycemic, antidiabetic, antioxidant, immunomodulatory, and antiulcer. Its unique properties endow it with a wide range of uses in food, beverage, pharmaceutical and industrial applications. It is used in the food industry as a stabilizer, emulsifier and thickening agent in icing, fillings, soft candy, chewing gum and other confectionery and to bind the sweeteners and flavourings in soft drinks. Its mixture of polysaccharides and glycoproteins gives it the properties of a glue and binder that is edible by humans. It still remains an important ingredient in soft drink syrup and "hard" gummy candies. It is also a traditional binder in watercolour paint, photography, gum printing, and as an ingredient in shoe polish.

    Palm kernel shells (PKS)

    Palm kernel shell (PKS) is a by-product of the palm oil industry but is fast becoming an important source of feedstock for biomass energy generation. Although always traditionally used by palm oil mills as a source of energy, interest from power generators is growing. Given its high energy content (14-20GJ/t) and the fact that it is a waste product, PKS is a relatively cheap source of biomass fuel. The introduction of subsidy support schemes for biomass use in Japan and South Korea in 2012 hugely increased demand for the fuel. Imports of PKS in countries in South East Asia have risen significantly since some countries there introduced new renewable energy subsidies. Until now the trade of PKS for energy has been opaque and hard to track. Supply has seemed plentiful, but at times perhaps unreliable.

    Charcoal

    Charcoal is a pure carbon also called char, that is made by cooking wood in a low oxygen setting. Charcoal is used in some toothpastes and used for cooking food in various forms. It also provides a low-cost method of cooking for local communities around the developing part of the world. In its activated form, it is a fine, odourless, black powder often used in emergency rooms to treat overdoses. Its toxin-absorbing properties have a wide range of medicinal and cosmetic uses.
